Reflection on Validation

During the validation process, I found several minor issues on the Feedback Page. Some form labels were not correctly associated with their input controls. I corrected this by ensuring that every label had a for attribute matching the id of its related input, select or textarea element.

I also checked the nesting of HTML elements, closing tags, image alternative text, heading order and internal links across my Feedback, Team and Content Pages. After making the required corrections, I validated each page again until the validator reported no errors.

The validation process was a valuable learning experience because it demonstrated the importance of semantic HTML, correct document structure and accessibility best practices. Running the W3C Validator regularly during development helped me identify problems early and prepare my Student 3 pages for the final coursework submission.
